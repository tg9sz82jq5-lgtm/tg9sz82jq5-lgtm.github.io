  • E.V.I. (Experimental Video Interface)
    June 16, 2026

    Featured in the exhibition "Death of the Music Video(?)"

    Semester-long project

    Tags: Intervention, Experimental, Hardware

    [...] Experimental Video Interface takes the position of an intervention in the music video industry. The interface consists out of multiple components chained together in a modular setup. These are made to explore the ambiguity of electrical signals used to transfer sound and image. It takes inspiration from dub music, which uses a mixing board like an instrument and revolves around reworking existing music. It is made to bring the personality and emotions of music to a frictionless video editing industry. All components and research are open-sourced through the website found below.

    Created within the brief "Death of the Music Video(?)" of Studio Digital Native at Design Academy Eindhoven, where students deconstruct the music video format, understanding it as a portal to today's digital worlds.

  • Techno-Exponentialism Existentialism
    March 9, 2026

    Collaborated with: Yunseo Cha, Lilly Van Bakel

    3-week-long project

    Tags: Datamoshing, Command-Line Video Editing, Music Video

    [...] Techno-Exponentialism, founded in Moore's Law, has been transforming the way we interact with our resources. It becomes ever more extractionist, often at the expense of our nature and well-being. The music video Techno-Exponentialism Existentialism explores these themes by exploring the exponential development of technology, becoming more and more dissonant, yet intriguing.

    Created as a kick-off assignment within Studio Digital Native at Design Academy Eindhoven, where students analyze one of the most influencial music videos, using it as inspiration for a contemporary approach to music videos.

  • Connecting Heritage
    June 18, 2026

    Semester-long project

    Tags: System, Product Proposition, Repurposing

    [...] Connecting Heritage is a modular outdoor bench system for UNESCO heritage sites. It aims to be placed at heritage sites that recently broke down, and is in the process of being restored. For the restoration, UNESCO aims to involve the local community of craftspeople and engineers to realise it. The structure exists mostly out of concrete, wherein remaining rubble of a broken heritage site is mixed. It creates a bench that is fully emerged in this site, and connects the community around this site.

    Rather than creating a finished product, Connecting Heritage is a website that gives measurements to local engineers. This way, different views on craft and architecture can be implemented, depending on the heritage sites. Still, some examples of the bench in context are given.

    Created in 'A hands-on Design Lab', a course at Design Academy Eindhoven, where students dissect a certain material or production method, and create a concept to pitch to a chosen client.

  • Have a Great Day!
    June 23, 2025

    Semester-long project

    Tags: Intervention, Graphic Design, Activism

    [...] Have a Great Day is a series of posters and stickers made to raise your voice. These posters were designed to confront people with the different languages within Brussels. It strives for inclusivity between the communities. This is done by teaching words of kindness from one to another spoken language. The context in which the posters were hung up is equally important. It is hung in places where these institutionalized languages are visible, and where there needs more acceptance to other languages. The stickers were made to give people a voice. They represent each community and which language they speak. The posters and stickers can be obtained for free and hung around the city, to give space for each language. This way, the city becomes more of a mutual ground between these languages, and people who see them feel more welcomed.

    Created within the semester topic of 'Friction' at Studio Technogeographies of Design Academy Eindhoven. The studio is a theoretical and practical laboratory placing design and designers as active agents for imagining potential future scenarios inside our contemporary technogeographies.

About Me

Jaan Van Rompaey is a Belgian designer and artist. His background is in industrial design, architecture, and fine arts, which has founded his approach to design. Digital media are the foundation of his projects, exploring narratives related to contemporary culture and society. Interventions and activistic approaches are often the basis of his work.

He often uses a conceptual approach to design, but keeps close contact with materialisation and physicality.
